Nicholas Griffen

Language Researcher

I am currently a PhD student at Université Paris Cité under the supervision of Ira Noveck. In the Fall of 2025, I plan to submit my doctoral thesis on "What Makes the Processing of Idioms Unique?".

Prior to beginning my doctoral studies in Paris, I was awarded a Master's Degree in Linguistics at Universität Göttingen. Under the supervision of Clemens Steiner-Mayr, I completed a thesis on the topic of "The Vagueness of Indeterminate Gradable Adjectives". During this project, I rapidly developed a strong interest in Experimental Pragmatics.

Subsequently, I was awarded a SMARTS-UP mobility scholarship and a LabEx Master’s Thesis Grant which led me to begin empirical research on language processing at Université Paris Cité.

During my PhD, we’ve implemented a variety of experimental techniques to investigate idiom processing. In the beginning of this project, the tasks we assigned were largely internet-based experiments (e.g., self-paced reading & norming tasks). Presently, I am in the midst of conducting an electroencephalogram (EEG) experiment to gain insight on the neural correlates of idiom processing.

In addition, I’ve led a bi-weekly Experimental Linguistics Workshop which takes place at the Laboratoire Linguistique Formelle and co-instructed a course on Psycholinguistics with Barbara Hemforth.
